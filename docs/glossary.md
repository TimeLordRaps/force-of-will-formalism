# Glossary

## Agent

A person, system, institution, AI, project, hypernode, or other entity that can be modeled as having structured agency.

## Hypernode

A local recursive agency-node that can be modeled through the six-will structure.

## Will

Structured agency across six projections:

```txt
W = <P, N, F, I, V, R>
```

Will is not mere desire. It is the structured relation among trace, live verification, future attractor, conserved structure, adaptation, and relation.

## Force of Will

The achieved coherence of a six-component will object:

```txt
FoW = C(W)
```

Force of Will is not domination or stubbornness. It is coherent agency across temporal, invariant, adaptive, and relational dimensions.

## Past Will `P`

The evidential trace of agency: what has been enacted, endured, chosen, repeated, abandoned, preserved, damaged, repaired, or left unresolved.

Short form:

```txt
Past will is evidence.
```

## Present Will `N`

Live agency under current verification: what is choosing, refusing, attending, clarifying, consenting, repairing, initiating, withholding, or redirecting now.

Short form:

```txt
Present will is verification.
```

## Now-Will

A clearer name for present will. The symbol `N` avoids conflict with `P`, which already denotes past will.

## Future Will `F`

The attractor-structure of agency: what an agent is moving toward, designing for, protecting, selecting, preparing, refusing to abandon, or making imaginable.

Short form:

```txt
Future will is attractor.
```

## Invariant Will `I`

Conserved agency-structure across change. It is what remains directionally recognizable across changing states, contexts, pressures, roles, interpretations, and adaptations.

Short form:

```txt
Invariant will is conserved structure.
```

## Variable Will `V`

Adaptive agency under changing conditions. It changes across state, context, pressure, information, embodiment, desire, opportunity, constraint, and social condition.

Short form:

```txt
Variable will is adaptation.
```

## Self-Adaptational Will `V_self`

The portion of variable will adapting to internal state, embodiment, energy, cognition, emotion, desire, health, attention, skill, information, and constraint.

## Social-Adaptational Will `V_social`

The portion of variable will adapting to other agents, audiences, institutions, incentives, norms, threats, support, status-pressure, recognition, misrepresentation, and consent-boundaries.

Important distinction:

```txt
Social-adaptational will does not make social reception identity.
```

It only means social conditions can affect adaptive behavior.

## Relational Will `R`

The coherence topology among the other will components. It determines how past, present, future, invariant, and variable will constrain, correct, reinforce, distort, resonate, or repair one another.

Short form:

```txt
Relational will is coherence topology.
```

## Coherence Functional `C`

A function, score, procedure, judgment, or model that evaluates the coherence of a will-object.

Possible dimensions include:

```txt
non-contradiction
mutual reinforcement
repair capacity
resistance to coercive collapse
adaptation without identity-loss
future orientation without delusion
past integration without domination
present verification without naivety
```

## Evidence

A past trace that can inform present verification, risk-modeling, and prediction without becoming destiny.

## Verification

Present-tense reality-checking of inherited assumptions, current actions, claimed meanings, and possible paths.

## Attractor

A future-oriented structure that pulls agency toward some protected possibility, capability, identity, repair, or becoming.

## Conserved Structure

What remains directionally recognizable across change without freezing the agent into rigidity.

## Adaptation

Change in agency under state, context, pressure, information, embodiment, desire, opportunity, constraint, or social condition.

## Coherence Topology

The relation-structure among components that determines whether the agency-object becomes integrated, fragmented, dominated, captured, or repaired.

## Unjust Degradation

A reduction of personhood, refusal-capacity, dignity, capability, repair, speech, belonging, or discovery in order to sustain a whole.

This should be distinguished from warranted de-ranking or de-legitimation of authority, rank, influence, or power when those positions are sustained through deception, coercion, or misuse.

## Non-Violation Floor

A baseline constraint: beings should not be violated, degraded, coerced, or erased merely because they have low value to a particular agent's sphere of action.

## Will-Recognition Floor

A baseline recognition that another entity has will or agency-structure, without requiring trust, admiration, obedience, access, intimacy, or positive regard.

## Consent Boundary

A constraint on relational will and social-adaptational will. Influence becomes illegitimate when it bypasses or degrades the target's ability to refuse.

## Identity Capture

A failure mode where external reception, social pressure, or misrepresentation gets mistaken for core identity or invariant will.

## Authority Without Re-Verification

A failure mode where past will dominates present will so strongly that current evidence and agency cannot correct it.

## Presentist Naivety

A failure mode where present will erases past evidence without risk-modeling.

## Coercive Vision

A failure mode where future will justifies present harm by appealing to an unverified destiny.

## Identity-Prison

A failure mode where invariant will becomes too rigid, too externally assigned, or too overfit to past evidence.

## Variable Chaos

A failure mode where adaptation loses relation to invariant will, evidence, verification, future attractor, or relational coherence.
