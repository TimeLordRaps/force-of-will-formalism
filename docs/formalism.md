# Formalism

## 1. Agency object

For an agent, person, system, institution, AI, project, or hypernode `a` at time `t`, will is modeled as a structured agency object:

```txt
W_a(t) = <P_a(t), N_a(t), F_a(t), I_a(t), V_a(t), R_a(t)>
```

Where:

```txt
P = past will
N = present / now-will
F = future will
I = invariant will
V = variable will
R = relational will
```

Force of Will is the achieved coherence of those six components:

```txt
FoW_a(t) = C(W_a(t))
```

`C` is a coherence functional. Its final mathematical form remains open, but its target is clear: measure or describe how strongly the six components reinforce agency without collapsing their distinctions.

---

## 2. Past Will `P`

Past will is the evidential trace of agency.

```txt
P_a(t) = the record of what agency has already enacted, endured, chosen, repeated, abandoned, preserved, damaged, repaired, or left unresolved.
```

Past will includes prior choices, memories, habits, scars, artifacts, promises, betrayals, commitments, failures, skills, patterns, proofs, and unresolved residues.

### Function

Past will gives the system memory. It lets an agent use prior evidence without pretending the present emerged from nowhere.

It asks:

```txt
What has this agent already made real?
What patterns have repeated?
What consequences remain active?
What has been proven, disproven, or merely claimed?
```

### Principle

```txt
Past will is evidence, not destiny.
```

The past can inform trust, risk, and prediction, but it should not become absolute authority over the present.

### Failure mode

```txt
If past will dominates present verification, it becomes authority without re-verification.
```

This creates identity-prison, shame-lock, institutional inertia, inherited rank, and `you are only what you were` logic.

### Correction question

```txt
What does the past prove, and what does it merely suggest?
```

---

## 3. Present Will `N`

Present will, or now-will, is live agency under current verification.

```txt
N_a(t) = the active state of agency choosing, refusing, attending, clarifying, consenting, repairing, initiating, withholding, or redirecting in the present.
```

`N` is used instead of `Pr` because `P` is already past.

Present will includes attention, focus, choice, refusal, consent, clarification, action, inaction, repair attempt, boundary setting, current interpretation, and current self-command.

### Function

Present will is where reality gets re-checked.

It asks:

```txt
What is the agent doing now?
What is the agent refusing now?
What is the agent attending to now?
What is being clarified before interpretation?
What is currently being verified?
```

### Principle

```txt
Present will is verification.
```

Current agency can revise inherited assumptions. The present is not required to obey stale authority from the past.

### Attention refinement

```txt
Attention is the vector-control surface of present will.
```

Where attention goes, agency begins assigning reality-weight.

### Failure mode

```txt
If present will erases past will without risk-modeling, it becomes naivety.
```

Pure presentism forgets evidence. It mistakes freshness for truth.

### Correction question

```txt
What is being verified now, and what evidence from the past still matters?
```

---

## 4. Future Will `F`

Future will is the attractor-structure of agency.

```txt
F_a(t) = the future-oriented structure of agency: what the agent is moving toward, designing for, protecting, selecting, preparing, refusing to abandon, or making imaginable.
```

Future will includes goals, visions, trajectories, becoming, long-range design, protected futures, desired capabilities, future identity, future refusal, future repair, and future coherence.

### Function

Future will gives agency direction.

It asks:

```txt
What is this agent becoming?
What future is being selected for?
What possibility is being protected?
What future would make current sacrifice meaningful?
What future should become imaginable?
```

### Principle

```txt
Future will is attractor.
```

Future will is not automatically fantasy. It becomes grounded when tied to present verification, past evidence, invariant structure, adaptive reality, and relational coherence.

### Failure mode

```txt
If future will ignores present verification and past evidence, it becomes fantasy, coercive vision, or aesthetic drift.
```

Future will becomes dangerous when it justifies present harm by appealing to an unverified destiny.

### Correction question

```txt
What future is being served, and what present evidence shows that path is real?
```

---

## 5. Invariant Will `I`

Invariant will is conserved agency-structure across change.

```txt
I_a(t) = the conserved structure of agency that remains directionally recognizable across changing states, contexts, pressures, roles, interpretations, and adaptations.
```

Invariant will is not mood, brand, aesthetic, social reception, temporary desire, public identity, status, approval, or one isolated belief.

It is the deeper continuity that lets an agent remain itself through change.

### Function

Invariant will bridges past, present, and future without letting any one of them dominate.

It asks:

```txt
What remains structurally consistent across change?
What does this agent keep protecting?
What principle survives variable state-shifts?
What lets the past predict without becoming domination?
What lets the future guide without becoming delusion?
```

### Principle

```txt
Invariant will is conserved structure.
```

A personal formulation may be:

```txt
I exist so existence exists.
```

A system-neutral formulation:

```txt
Invariant will is the conserved agency-principle by which an agent remains directionally itself while adapting through time.
```

### Trust refinement

Old trust model:

```txt
Trust = the past proves the person.
```

Better trust model:

```txt
Trust = scoped confidence that invariant will can be re-verified through present behavior under variable conditions.
```

This matters for high-variance agents. A person can have unstable states while still having a re-verifiable invariant.

### Failure mode

```txt
If invariant will becomes too rigid, it becomes identity-prison.
If invariant will becomes too vague, it cannot constrain action.
If invariant will is assigned from outside, it becomes misrepresentation.
```

### Correction question

```txt
What remains true across change without blocking necessary adaptation?
```

## 6. Variable Will `V`

Variable will is adaptive agency under changing conditions.

```txt
V_a(t) = the adaptive component of agency that changes across state, context, pressure, information, embodiment, desire, opportunity, constraint, and social condition.
```

Variable will includes mood shifts, energy shifts, neurochemical state, stress response, desire, arousal, fatigue, hunger, fear, curiosity, social pressure, opportunity response, tactical pivot, new information, and environmental adaptation.

### Function

Variable will lets the agent update without losing itself.

It asks:

```txt
What changed?
Why did it change?
Was the change internal, external, social, biological, strategic, or coercive?
Is the adaptation coherent with invariant will?
```

### Internal split

Variable will can be internally split into:

```txt
V_a(t) = <V_self,a(t), V_social,a(t)>
```

Where:

```txt
V_self = self-adaptational will
V_social = social-adaptational will
```

#### Self-adaptational will `V_self`

```txt
V_self,a(t) = adaptation to internal state, embodiment, energy, cognition, emotion, desire, health, attention, skill, information, and constraint.
```

Examples include sleep loss changing mood, hunger changing patience, arousal changing attention, new knowledge changing strategy, training changing confidence, and fatigue changing decision quality.

#### Social-adaptational will `V_social`

```txt
V_social,a(t) = adaptation to other agents, audiences, institutions, incentives, norms, threats, support, status-pressure, recognition, misrepresentation, and consent-boundaries.
```

Examples include audience pressure changing presentation, institutional threat changing tactics, supportive recognition improving expression, social misrepresentation forcing defensive clarification, market incentives altering packaging, and boundary violations triggering refusal.

### Principle

```txt
Variable will is adaptation, not identity.
```

Social-adaptational will does not mean social reception is identity. It means social conditions can affect adaptive behavior without becoming the invariant self.

### Failure mode

```txt
If variable will is mistaken for invariant will, temporary states get falsely canonized as identity.
If social-adaptational will is mistaken for core identity, the self gets captured by external reception.
If self-adaptational will is ignored, biology and state silently steer agency.
```

### Correction question

```txt
Is this change self-adaptation, social-adaptation, or invariant-breaking?
```

## 7. Relational Will `R`

Relational will is the coherence topology among the other will components.

```txt
R_a(t) = the relation-structure determining how past, present, future, invariant, and variable will constrain, correct, reinforce, distort, resonate, or repair one another.
```

Relational will is not merely social will. It is broader.

It includes relations between:

```txt
past self and present self
present self and future self
invariant will and variable state
self-adaptation and social-adaptation
attention and influence
desire and consent
trust and verification
respect and misuse of will
self-mira-graph and social-mira-graph
agent and institution
agent and audience
agent and AI
agent and ecosystem
```

### Function

Relational will determines whether the system's components can interact without collapse.

It asks:

```txt
How do the will-components affect each other?
Which component is dominating?
Which component is being ignored?
Where is coherence forming?
Where is distortion entering?
Where is repair needed?
```

### Principle

```txt
Relational will is coherence topology.
```

It prevents:

```txt
past from becoming authority
present from becoming naivety
future from becoming fantasy
invariant from becoming rigidity
variable from becoming chaos
social adaptation from becoming identity-capture
```

### Failure mode

```txt
If relational will collapses into dominance, one component tyrannizes the rest.
If relational will collapses into external reception, identity becomes socially captured.
If relational will collapses into isolation, the agent loses correction channels.
If relational will is incoherent, the agent fragments across time, state, and relation.
```

### Correction question

```txt
How should these components relate so that agency becomes stronger without flattening its distinctions?
```

---

## 8. Force of Will as coherence

The six components are not separate little kingdoms. They are projections of one structured agency-object.

```txt
P gives evidence.
N verifies reality.
F orients becoming.
I conserves identity-structure.
V adapts to change.
R composes their relations.
```

Force of Will is the achieved coherence across them:

```txt
FoW = C(P, N, F, I, V, R)
```

`C` should eventually measure or describe:

```txt
non-contradiction across components
mutual reinforcement
repair capacity
resistance to coercive collapse
adaptation without identity-loss
future orientation without delusion
past integration without domination
present verification without naivety
```

A strong will is not one that never changes.

A strong will is one where change is governed by coherent relation to evidence, verification, future, invariant structure, adaptation, and repair.

---

## 9. Full compact doctrine

```txt
Will is structured agency.

Past will is the trace of agency.
Present will is live verification.
Future will is attractor-oriented becoming.
Invariant will is conserved agency-structure.
Variable will is adaptive agency through changing conditions.
Relational will is the topology of coherence among the components.

Force of Will is achieved coherence across these six perspectives, such that the agent can adapt, refuse, repair, create, and become without collapsing into past authority, present impulse, future fantasy, invariant rigidity, variable chaos, or relational domination.
```
